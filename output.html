Word

/ 'pro-noun-si-as-shion/
Word1 - (noun)
noun: word; plural noun: words
  1. a unit of language that native speakers can identify
    Ex: He hardly said ten words all morning.
  2. a brief statement
    Ex: He didn't say a word about it
  3. information about recent and important events
    Ex: There is word that the Maple Leafs plan on trading Marner for Stamkos
Word2 - (verb)
verb: word; past tense: worded; past participle: worded; gerund or present participle: wording; 3rd person present: words;
  1. to put into words or an expression
    Ex: Word it
Word3 - (Urban Dictionary)
  1. "Word" is the shortened form of the phrase: "[my word is my bond]" which was originated by inmates in U.S. [prisons]. The longer phrase was shortened to "[word is bond]" before becoming "word," which is most commonly used. It basically means "truth." Or "to speak the truth."
    Ex: "Yo, I destroyed [twelve] boxers last night." "Word?" ("You speak [the truth]?") "Word." ("[I speak the truth].")
